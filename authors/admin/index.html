Postdoctoral Research Associate II

University of Arizona/Steward Obersvatory

test I am a postdoctoral scholar at University of Arizona/Steward Observatory, working with Prof. Erika Hamden since 2019 Fall. Before I join Steward Observatory, I did my Ph.D. at Seoul National University (SNU), Republic of Korea. I was also a research assistant/student fellow in the School of Physics at Korea Institute for Advanced Study (KIAS). My Ph.D. advisors were Prof. Yong-Sun Park at SNU and Prof. Changbom Park at KIAS. I have worked with the instrumentation group (Head: Prof. A. N. Ramaprakash) at Inter-University Centre for Astronomy and Astrophysics (IUCAA) in Pune, India during my Ph.D.

My research focus is on astronomical instrumentation and observational astronomy, in particular building UV/Visible telescope and instrument, and studying the kinematics of galaxies. Currently, I am involved and working on projects about 1) a new concept of UV space telescope that will let us take an image of coronal gas of nearby galaxy halos, 2) a ground-based multi-object spectrograph for the purpose of surveying the redshift of galaxies in the local universe, and 3) preparation of the second flight of FIREBall-2 UV balloon telescope. Previously I was heavily involved in the development of optical multi-IFU spectrograph (DOTIFS) before and I am still working on the development of its reduction software. I am also using SDSS-IV MaNGA IFU survey data to study environmental effects on galaxy kinematics.
